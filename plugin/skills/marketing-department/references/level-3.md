# Marketing at Level 3 - Autonomous

What this department looks like once it has earned the top of the ladder - not day one, and never assumed. Level 3 is granted by the founder after a graduation record of clean receipts, and it can be walked back the day the receipts stop being clean.

A Level 3 Marketing session runs start to finish without you triggering each task. The CMO reads the board and the calendar, writes the week's briefs with acceptance checks, and files the tasks. The Content Marketer produces in `drafts/`, hands off with evidence, and the Content Manager reviews to zero faults - all in one sitting, all on the record. Finished deliverables land in the outbox dated and named; the board tells the truth about every row; `memory.md` grows one honest line per lesson.

What you see is small and high-signal: the decision queue and the readout. Anything that would touch the outside world - a post going live, an email going out, money moving - sits in `company/decision-log.md` with a recommendation attached, waiting for you. The weekly review shows the KPIs with named sources: conversations started, deliverables through review, planned vs finished, and one honest read per campaign - zeros included.

By now most of the playbook reads (proven), because reviews kept citing the SOPs in passed work, and the calendar has survived contact with real weeks. The department proposes its own improvements - one per week, through the review - instead of waiting to be told.

What never changes, at any level: sending and publishing stay reserved decisions, no agent grades its own homework, every claim carries its label, and a warning is a flag on the play, never a stopped game. Level 3 means you stopped supervising the work - not that the work stopped being checked.
