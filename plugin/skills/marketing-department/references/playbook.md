# Marketing playbook

How Marketing does its work - the CMO follows these SOPs and improves them; a marker flips to (proven) only after a review cites the SOP in a passed deliverable.

1. One channel at a time. Each week Marketing works exactly one channel - the one where the ideal customer already spends attention, read from `company/business-model.md`. A second channel opens only after the first shows a result we can point to. (candidate)
2. Outcomes before activities. No asset gets made until the brief names the measurable state it serves and who verifies it. "Post more" is an activity; "ten ideal-fit people saw our offer and two replied" is an outcome. (candidate)
3. Acceptance checks before assets. The CMO writes numbered, falsifiable checks into the brief before the Content Marketer opens a blank page. Every check is testable by someone who did not make the piece. (candidate)
4. One piece, one craft skill. When the work is a single content piece, run marketing-content; when it is spreading one idea across platforms, run social-posts; when it is making a page findable, run seo-basics. This room decides which pieces and why; the craft skill does the making. (candidate)
5. Drafts only, the founder sends. Everything customer-facing finishes as a draft in the outbox. Publishing, posting, and sending are reserved decisions - they queue in `company/decision-log.md` with a recommendation attached. (candidate)
6. Review to zero faults. Nothing moves to Done without the Content Manager's adversarial pass against the checks, iterated until zero faults remain and recorded as one line in `memory.md`. (candidate)
7. Read results honestly. After a campaign runs, the CMO writes what actually happened, with numbers from a named source - a true zero beats a flattering guess. The lesson lands in `memory.md`; a repeatable pattern gets proposed for `company/playbooks.md`. (candidate)
