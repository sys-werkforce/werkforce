# Strategy at Level 3 - Autonomous

What this department looks like once it has earned the top of the ladder - not day one, and never assumed. Level 3 is granted by the founder after a graduation record of clean receipts, and it can be walked back the day the receipts stop being clean.

A Level 3 Strategy session runs start to finish without you triggering each task. The Chief Strategy Officer opens with the market watch, records what changed with sources, and picks the session's teardown target from the watchlist. The Strategy Specialist builds the dossier in `drafts/` with every claim sourced and dated; the Strategy Manager reviews it adversarially, downgrades what the evidence cannot carry, and lands one cited pattern in `company/playbooks.md` - all in one sitting, all on the record.

What you see is small and high-signal. The pattern library grows by one honest entry per session, and other rooms actually use it: Marketing's briefs cite a pricing pattern, the CEO seat's evaluations point at a P{n} instead of a hunch, and the quarterly strategy review reads a shelf of receipts instead of a stack of impressions. When the evidence points at a real move - a price change, a repositioning, a new offer - it arrives in `company/decision-log.md` as a queued decision with the dossier attached, waiting for the CEO seat's evaluation and your verdict. Nothing waits on you silently, and nothing moves without you.

By now the watchlist has survived contact with real quarters - stale names retired with a dated note, new alternatives added when customers mention them - and most of the playbook reads (proven), because reviews kept citing the SOPs in passed dossiers. The department proposes its own improvements, one per week, through the review.

What never changes, at any level: every claim carries its label and its source, patterns without evidence wait, verdicts belong to you, no agent grades its own homework, and a warning is a flag on the play, never a stopped game. Level 3 means you stopped supervising the reading of the market - not that the market stopped being read with receipts.
