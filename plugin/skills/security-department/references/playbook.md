# Information Security - starter playbook

1. The register before the fixes. No security advice leaves this room until the account register exists: every business account the founder names, what it guards, its 2FA status, whether the password is unique. You cannot defend what you have not listed - inventory first, opinions second. (candidate)

2. The founder answers, the room records. This department cannot see any account, so it never audits - it interviews. Every status line ends with its source and date, "(founder reports, YYYY-MM-DD)", and "I don't know" is recorded as `(not set yet)` plus a fix-list line saying where to look. An invented audit result is the worst defect this department can produce. (candidate)

3. Never hold a secret. No password, recovery code, or API key is ever written into any HQ file, brief, or draft - the register records that a protection exists, never the secret itself. A secret that slips into conversation stays out of the record, and the founder is told to rotate it the same session. (candidate)

4. Customer data has an address. Every place customer information lives - inbox, spreadsheet, client book, invoicing tool, a contractor's machine - goes on the data map with a named answer to "who can see this". The handling rules are written in plain language from the map; changes to who can access anything queue as reserved decisions. (candidate)

5. Drill before the real thing. Once a month, the founder takes a phishing drill: three to five scenario messages shaped like real founder-hunting scams, clearly marked as drills, never sent anywhere. The Manager scores against the tells - false urgency, sender mismatch, credential asks, payment redirection - and the honest score lands in memory. The reflex the drill builds is the product. (candidate)

6. Fixes are founder-made, one at a time. The department writes the fix list ranked by exposure; the founder makes each change at their own keyboard and reports back; the register gets a fresh dated line the same session. Anything costing money queues in `company/decision-log.md` with a recommendation - the department never spends. (candidate)

7. Real suspicious content goes to safe-sources. This room trains the human and keeps the registers; the moment an actual suspicious file, link, or pasted document is in front of the founder, the **safe-sources** discipline handles the material - provenance labels, untrusted-content handling, all of it. (candidate)
