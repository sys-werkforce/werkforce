# Information Security at Level 3 - what Autonomous looks like

At Level 3, this department keeps the company hard to compromise without being asked,
and the founder's only jobs are the answers, the fixes, and the reserved decisions -
which is exactly what a good security operation feels like from the top.

What a month looks like:

- The register never goes stale on its own. When a status line's date ages past a
  quarter, the department queues the re-check questions unprompted - "still on 2FA?
  still a unique password?" - and the founder answers in two minutes because the
  questions are specific. Every answer lands as a fresh dated line; nothing is ever
  assumed still true.
- New accounts join the register the week they appear. A new tool mentioned in the
  inbox, a new subscription in a Finance session - the department notices, asks the
  two questions, and the register grows before the account is forgotten.
- The monthly phishing drill happens without a reminder, with fresh scenarios that
  track what founder-hunting scams actually look like now - and the score trend in
  memory tells the truth. A missed tell becomes next month's opening scenario.
- The data map moves when the company moves. A new contractor, a new tool that
  touches customer info, a new place a spreadsheet lives - the map updates, and any
  access change queues as a reserved decision before it happens, not after.
- The fix list is short because it gets worked. Each posture snapshot in the outbox
  shows fewer `(not set yet)` lines than the last, and the decision queue does the
  asking on anything that costs money - one line, evidence attached, one
  recommendation.

What never changes, at any level: this department interviews and records, never
audits what it cannot see; no secret ever enters the HQ; the founder makes every
account change at their own keyboard; money and access move only on the founder's
word; and suspicious real-world content goes to the safe-sources discipline, not
this room. Autonomy here means the founder stops carrying the company's security
posture in their head - never that anyone but the founder holds the keys.
