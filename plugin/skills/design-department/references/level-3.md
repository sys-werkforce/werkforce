# Design at Level 3 - what Autonomous looks like

At Level 3, Design runs its own session without you in the room and you would
still recognize every piece it produces from across the street. That is the
whole test: consistency without supervision.

What a Level 3 Design session looks like from the record:

- The session opens on the wake run, reads its own charter and board, and picks
  its work from Filed rows and the drift watch - no founder nudge needed.
- The brand kit is the standing authority. New assets cite it, reviews compare
  against it, and when the company's voice shifts in `company/profile.md`, the
  kit gets a refresh brief filed the same week, on the department's own
  initiative.
- Templates cover every recurring shape the company produces - documents, decks,
  social posts - and other departments pull from the outbox as a matter of
  habit. Marketing does not ask what the header looks like; it lifts the header.
- Briefs carry acceptance checks a stranger could test, the Design Specialist
  ships self-contained files with honest claim labels, and the Design Manager
  reviews side by side to zero faults before any row reads Done. Most sessions,
  every check passes on the first review; when one fails, the fix lands the
  same session and the lesson lands in `records/improvements.md`.
- The monthly drift audit runs without being asked: three company pieces pulled,
  set against the kit, mismatches turned into fix tasks with memory lines.
- The only moments that reach your desk are reserved ones - a public-facing look
  change, a font worth paying for, an asset ready to send - each queued in
  `company/decision-log.md` with a recommendation already attached.

What it took to get here, per the graduation record: weeks of Supervised then
Trusted sessions, receipts on every Done row, reviews by a seat that never
built the work, and not one invented fact about your business along the way.
The department earned the room's trust the same way a person would - by being
checkable, and by being checked.
