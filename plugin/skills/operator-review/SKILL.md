# Operator review - the founder's sign-off walk, one artifact at a time

## When to use
The founder wants to clear the tasks that passed Manager review and now wait at the Operator review stage for their own sign-off. Wake phrases: "werkforce, sign-offs", "operator review", "walk my sign-off queue". Boundary: founders-desk owns the seven reserved decision types (external sends, spending, pricing, public claims, hiring and firing, deleting work, ratifying visions and strategy) - this skill owns only the work-product sign-off walk, the founder's review of a completed deliverable that a department has finished and the Reviewer has passed. Boundary: review-desk and the department Reviewer own Manager review - the certification that the work is correct; this skill starts only after that gate is passed and a row sits at Operator review. A sign-off walk that starts ratifying a vision or approving a spend has left its lane and belongs at the desk.

## What it produces
A walked queue and, per decision the founder actually speaks, one appended line in records/operator-reviews.md plus one board row moved to match. Nothing else is written. For each row presented: the task name, the department, how long it has waited, the Reviewer's Manager-review verdict, and the finished deliverable (the outbox .html) linked one click away so the founder reviews the actual work product, not a path (SOP 11, the founder's link-artifacts rule). A signed-off task moves to Done with a receipt naming what was produced, where it lives, and who reviewed. A sent-back task reopens to In progress with the operator's verbatim reason noted, its history intact. The audit log gets one line per decision. This skill appends to the operator-reviews ledger and edits the named board cells only - it writes no deliverable and touches no other department's record.

## Steps
1. Read the facts fresh from their authorities: every `departments/*/board.md` for rows whose Stage is exactly `Operator review`, and for each such row its brief in `departments/{slug}/briefs.md`, its Reviewer verdict in `departments/{slug}/memory.md`, and the deliverable pair its receipt names in `departments/{slug}/outbox/`. Never walk the queue from memory. If a receipt names a file, confirm the file exists before presenting the row; a receipt pointing at a missing artifact is a warning line to records/warnings.md, not a row the founder can sign off blind.
2. Order the queue oldest first by the row's Filed date - the founder walks the work that has waited longest before the work that just arrived. State the count up front ("3 tasks await your sign-off") so the founder knows the size of the walk.
3. Present each row one at a time, never as a batch to approve in bulk: task name, department, days waited, the Manager-review verdict quoted, and the outbox .html linked one click away. The founder reviews the actual delivered work product on their screen before deciding - a sign-off given without the artifact shown is exactly the failure SOP 14 exists to prevent. Wait for the founder's call on this row before moving to the next.
4. On a sign-off: append one line to records/operator-reviews.md in the ledger shape - `- YYYY-MM-DD [department] TASK-ID signed-off: {one line naming what the operator reviewed}` - then flip the board row's Stage to `Done` and write its Receipt cell (what was produced, where it lives, who reviewed). Done means operator-signed, per the board law and standing order 3. Log one `[review]` line to records/audit-log.md pointing at the ledger line.
5. On a send-back: capture the operator's reason verbatim before anything moves (standing order, founder 2026-07-23; SOP 15). If the founder gives a reason, append `- YYYY-MM-DD [department] TASK-ID sent-back: "{operator's reasons word for word}"`. If the founder offers none, ask for it once - the desk never silently accepts a reasonless decline. If the founder cannot give the reason yet, file the line flagged `sent-back: reason pending` and leave it for the morning run to recheck until the reason lands. Then reopen the board row: Stage back to `In progress`, its Receipt cell noting the send-back and its reason, its prior history left intact and never erased. Log one `[review]` audit line.
6. Feed the review-gap system, do not analyze it here. Every sent-back line - and especially its verbatim reason - is the raw material the review-gap analysis (`departments/operations/outbox/*-review-gap-analysis.md`) reads to distill what the Operator bar was looking for that the Manager bar missed. This skill's job is to capture that reason cleanly and completely; the analysis that reads the corpus is a separate deliverable and is not run from this walk. A reason captured as "declined" with no substance starves that system - which is why step 5 refuses it.
7. Close the walk with an honest tally: how many signed off, how many sent back, how many filed reason-pending for the morning run to chase. If the queue was empty, say so plainly - a true empty queue is a real result, not a gap.

## Boundaries
- Never signs off on the operator's behalf. The founder's spoken word is the only thing that appends a signed-off line; the skill presents and records, it does not decide.
- Never batches without showing each artifact. Every row is presented individually with its deliverable one click away; there is no "approve all."
- Never accepts a reasonless decline silently. A send-back without a reason is asked for once, then filed reason-pending and rechecked - never recorded as a bare "declined."
- Never erases history. A send-back reopens the row and annotates it; the ledger is append-only and the board row's prior receipts are not overwritten out of existence.
- founders-desk stays the home of the seven reserved decision types. This skill owns only the work-product sign-off walk and defers any reserved-decision ask to the desk.

## Passing the standard's four hostile tests
1. **Evidence substitution** - if this skill's on-screen presentation vanished, the outcome is still fully verifiable from durable files: every decision is an append-only line in records/operator-reviews.md, every board row carries its Stage and Receipt, and the audit log points at both. The skill's proof lives in ledgers, not in its own output.
2. **Vague excellence** - each promise is falsifiable. "Oldest first" is checkable against Filed dates; "one click away" is checkable against the linked outbox .html existing; "verbatim reason" is checkable against the sent-back line reading as the founder spoke it; "reopened, not erased" is checkable against the row sitting at In progress with its history present. A reviewer can prove any of these unmet.
3. **Scope creep** - the skill signs off finished work products only. It reopens no settled decision and reaches into no reserved territory: reserved decisions route to founders-desk, Manager review stays with the Reviewer, and the review-gap distillation stays a separate deliverable. It solves the one unstated problem it names and stops.
4. **Self-review** - the skill grades nothing of its own. It records the founder's verdict; it never certifies a deliverable, never overrides a Reviewer, and never marks its own walk correct. The review triangle holds - the operator is the reviewer here, and the skill is the clerk.

## Honest residual risk
The queue read depends on department boards spelling the Stage exactly `Operator review`; a row parked under a drifted stage name is invisible to this walk and would silently never reach the founder - werkforce-checkup's stage-vocabulary check is the backstop, not this skill. The reason-pending path relies on the morning run actually rechecking; if run-the-day is skipped, a deferred reason can sit uncaptured and the review-gap corpus stays thin. And the skill trusts that a receipt's named artifact is the artifact the founder should see - it confirms the file exists but cannot confirm the file is the right or current version, so a stale outbox render could be signed off as if fresh. The skill has not yet run against a live multi-row queue; its oldest-first ordering and one-at-a-time discipline are untested at volume.

## What good looks like
A strong walk lets the founder decide each task from the work itself in one motion: the deliverable is on their screen, the Manager verdict is quoted, and every send-back leaves the ledger carrying the reason in the founder's own words. A weak walk lists task names with paths the founder has to hunt down, approves in a batch, or files a decline as "sent back" with no reason - that walk has starved the review-gap system and broken the link-artifacts rule.

## How we know it worked
Every decision the founder spoke is one appended line in records/operator-reviews.md and one matching board move (signed-off -> Done with receipt; sent-back -> In progress with reason); every sent-back line carries either a verbatim reason or a live reason-pending flag; no row was presented without its deliverable linked; the queue was walked oldest-first; the audit log has one line per decision; and this skill wrote nothing outside the operator-reviews ledger, the board cells it moved, and the audit/warnings ledgers - no deliverable authored, no reserved decision touched.
