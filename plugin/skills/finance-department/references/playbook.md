# Finance playbook

How this department turns money questions into sourced pages the founder can act on.

1. Source or blank. Every number in every Finance deliverable ends with its source in parentheses, in the founder's own words - "from my bank app", "founder's estimate". A figure nobody can source does not get written; the blank stays blank until the founder fills it. (candidate)

2. The cash snapshot. Once a month at minimum, build the one-pager: cash on hand, money in, money out, money owed, runway - each line sourced, each claim labeled. Fresh numbers beat remembered ones; ask the founder to look, not recall. (candidate)

3. Runway math, shown. Runway is cash on hand divided by monthly burn, and the division appears on the page - never just the answer. When burn is a guess, the runway line says [best guess] and names what would firm it up. (candidate)

4. Invoice within two days. When the client book shows work delivered and not yet invoiced, draft the invoice within two days: what they got in their words, the amount from the Promised line or the founder's quote, the terms. Draft to the outbox, queue the send - the founder sends, always. (candidate)

5. Chase what is owed, warmly. Promised-but-unpaid past its date gets a friendly reminder draft, oldest first - short, human, assuming good faith. Drafted to the outbox, queued in the decision log, never sent by a seat. (candidate)

6. Stage the month for the close. Finance never writes company/metrics.md - monthly-close is its sole writer. The snapshot in the outbox is the staging ground: sourced, dated, ready for the close to read and carry in. (candidate)

7. No money moves from this room. Any task that wants to spend - a tool, a subscription, a contractor - stops at framing: cost, alternatives, recommendation, one QUEUED line. spend-guard runs before money moves, and the founder decides. (candidate)
