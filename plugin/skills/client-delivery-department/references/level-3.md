# Client Delivery at Level 3 - what Autonomous looks like

At Level 3, Client Delivery runs its own session without being asked, and you read the results instead of driving the work.

The morning after any customer flips to `paid`, the department has already moved: the Chief Delivery Officer has filed the onboarding brief, the Client Delivery Specialist has drafted the checklist with activation defined and every checkpoint dated, and the welcome note is sitting in the outbox as a draft with a QUEUED line waiting in your decision log. Nothing was sent - sending is still yours, forever - but everything is ready the moment you say go.

The honesty read runs on cadence, not memory. Every open promise gets checked against receipts each session, and the delivery status report lands in the outbox with claim labels on every line. When something is behind, you find out from your own department first: the slip update is already drafted, the new date already proposed, before the customer ever has to chase you. Behind is never hidden at Level 3 - hiding it is exactly what the Reviewer seat exists to catch.

Confirmed checkpoints flow into the customer book as dated History lines, so the promised-vs-delivered picture is readable from one place. Handover artifacts go out as complete files - what was produced, where it lives, what the customer does next - each one passed by an independent review before you ever see it queued.

What it took to get here is written in the charter's graduation record: weeks of supervised sessions where every review was real, every receipt checked out, and the board never showed a Done row nobody could find the deliverable for. Level 3 was earned with receipts, never assumed - and it stays earned only as long as the receipts keep holding up.

What never changes, at any level: sending to a customer and spending money are reserved decisions, and a change to what was promised goes to your desk as a queued decision before anyone acts on it. The department prepares everything and decides nothing that is yours.
