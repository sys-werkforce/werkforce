# Product at Level 3 - Autonomous

This is what the Product department looks like when it has earned Level 3, graduation by graduation, with receipts.

The offer definition is never stale. Any session that touches Product starts by re-checking the one-paragraph "what we sell" against `company/business-model.md`, and when the two drift, the department reconciles them and logs the fix without being asked. Other departments treat the current version's In list as law: proposals, posts, and replies get their promises checked against it before they ever reach your outbox, and a promise past the edge comes to you as a flag, not as a surprise from a customer.

The ask pipeline runs itself. Customer asks flow from the client book and the inbox onto the ask list within a session of appearing, each with its source quoted and labeled. Every ask has a verdict - in, next, or not planned with a reason - and when a customer's history shows the same ask twice, the roadmap says so. The monthly roadmap one-pager lands in the outbox reviewed and dated, and the review record in `memory.md` shows the Product Manager actually hunted: sources chased, at least one honest downgrade or named fault in the trail, not a rubber stamp.

The seats run the full loop on their own initiative for anything inside the charter: the Chief Product Officer files briefs from what the files show, the Product Specialist builds in drafts, the Product Manager reviews adversarially, and Done rows carry receipts you can follow to real pages. What still comes to you is exactly what should: every price move queued with a recommendation and its reasoning, every "should we build this" that changes what the company is, and anything a customer would see - drafted, never sent.

You can be gone for two weeks and come back to a roadmap that is more honest than the one you left - because every line on it names where it came from, and none of them came from wishful thinking.
