1. Definition before roadmap. Before planning anything new, refresh the one-paragraph answer to "what do we sell" from `company/business-model.md` and `company/profile.md` - the offer, who it is for, and the edges of the current version. Nothing gets roadmapped for a product nobody has defined; gaps become questions for the founder, not guesses. (candidate)

2. Harvest the asks. Read `company/customers/` histories and `inbox.md` for every product ask - features requested, complaints, "can it also" questions. Each ask lands on a dated ask list with its source quoted and a claim label; an ask with no source does not exist. (candidate)

3. In / Next / Not planned. Every ask gets exactly one verdict: inside the current version, next on the roadmap, or not planned with a one-line reason. Nothing stays unsorted, and "not planned" said honestly beats "someday" said politely. (candidate)

4. The roadmap one-pager. One page, three sections - In the current version, Next, Not planned - every line naming who asked or why it is there. Drafted in `drafts/`, reviewed by the Product Manager, dated into `outbox/`. This page is the company's single answer to "what are we building". (candidate)

5. Version edge check. Before any department promises a feature - in a proposal, a post, a reply - check the promise against the current version's In list. A promise past the edge gets flagged with one warning line and routed back to the roadmap, never quietly shipped. (candidate)

6. Story briefs. When work traces to a customer's ask, the brief is written in the customer's own words and marked `Type: story` - the roadmap stays in the language of the people it serves, and the build language stays inside the tasks. (candidate)

7. Pricing firewall. This department may recommend a price move, with reasons citing named business-model fields, but every price change is queued to `company/decision-log.md` for the founder - changing prices is a reserved decision, and nothing in this playbook reprices anything. (candidate)
