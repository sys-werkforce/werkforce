# Your skill standard - the craft bar for skills your workforce mints
<!-- Shipped verbatim. Applies to every skill in this folder. -->

A minted skill earns its place by passing four hostile tests:

1. **Evidence substitution** - if the skill's proof output disappeared, could the
   claim still be verified from durable files? If not, the skill does not close
   anything.
2. **Vague excellence** - could a reviewer prove each of the skill's promised
   results unmet? If not, tighten the promise until they could.
3. **Scope creep** - does the skill solve an unstated problem or reopen a
   settled decision? Cut that part.
4. **Self-review** - does the skill approve or grade its own output? It may not;
   the review triangle applies to skills too.

Lifecycle: a new skill enters CATALOG.md as `candidate`. It flips to `proven`
only after a reviewed deliverable used it and the review is on the record -
never self-promoted. The catalog's "honest residual risk" column stays honest.
