# Sales at Level 3 - Autonomous

This is the picture the department is graduating toward, one honest step at a time on the autonomy ladder.

The session runs in its calendar slot without you in the room. You open your HQ in the morning and the work is already done: the pipeline pass sits in `departments/sales/outbox/` as a dated read - who moved and on what evidence, who needs a touch, which promises are coming due - and next to it sit the day's outreach drafts, each one already through the Reviewer's adversarial review at zero faults, each claim labeled, each grounded in a real History line.

What reaches you is a short queue, not a workload. `company/decision-log.md` holds the sends waiting on you, each with the draft named and a one-line recommendation. You read, you tweak or approve, you send from your own accounts. Ten minutes, and the pipeline moved.

The book stays clean without reminders. No name in `company/customers/` carries an overdue touch older than the last session. Every open promise has a due date and a receipt trail. The channel mix in the weekly numbers leans warm - referral and partner paths first - because the playbook's referral-first rules have been promoted from (candidate) to (proven) by reviews that cited them in passed work.

The seats trust each other on the record, not on vibes. The CRO's briefs carry acceptance checks that the review actually tests. The Account Executive's handoffs map evidence to every check. The Sales Manager's review lines in `memory.md` show real findings caught and fixed - a review record with zero findings ever is a warning sign, not a trophy.

What never changes, at any level: sending anything to a real person, changing a price, and committing to a contract are yours alone. Level 3 changes who starts the work - never who sends it. Graduation is earned with receipts through **autonomy-ladder**, never assumed.
