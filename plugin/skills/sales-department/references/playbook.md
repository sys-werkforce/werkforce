# Sales playbook

How Sales turns interest into money without burning the trust it runs on.

1. **The pipeline pass.** Every session opens by walking every file in `company/customers/` - overdue touches first, then today's, then names with no next touch set. Three questions per name: does the evidence move the stage, who needs a touch, which open promise is coming due. The pass produces the session's work; nothing gets drafted that the pass did not surface. (candidate)

2. **Referrals before strangers.** The first question on any new-pipeline motion is "who already trusts us and could introduce us warmly?" Every happy customer at `delivered` is a warm-introduction ask waiting to be drafted; delivered work is the referral engine. Only when the warm answer is truly empty does a colder path get considered - and never as unsolicited volume. (candidate)

3. **Partnerships before volume.** Find the people who already serve your ideal customer with something adjacent, and propose something genuinely good for their audience - a shared piece, a mutual introduction, a bundled offer. One strong partner sends more real pipeline than a hundred cold messages, and keeps sending it. (candidate)

4. **Every touch is a draft.** Anything customer-facing is written in full, reviewed, and filed in `outbox/` with the founder's name on the send - sending anything to a real person outside the company is a reserved decision, always. A draft in the outbox is finished work; the send is a separate decision. (candidate)

5. **Qualify before you propose.** Before any proposal or price conversation, a qualification note in the customer's own words: what they need, by when, and what budget signals actually exist. A stage moves on what the customer said or did, never on optimism. (candidate)

6. **Promises outrank prospects.** An open promise to someone who paid, or is about to, outranks any new outreach. Every pipeline pass checks promised-versus-delivered before new motions get drafted - kept promises are where referrals come from. (candidate)
