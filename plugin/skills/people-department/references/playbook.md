# People & Talent - starter playbook

1. Brief before work. No human starts work for the company without a written brief: the outcome in plain words, the deliverable, numbered acceptance checks, the deadline, and the agreed rate. A contractor who can misread the scope will - the brief exists so they cannot. (candidate)

2. Check the rate before you recommend it. Every rate recommendation carries at least two comparison points, each with a named source - a marketplace listing, a past invoice, a quote the founder received. An unsourced number is labeled `[best guess]` and says so; a blank beats an invented figure. **research-desk** owns the sourcing discipline when the hunt goes outside. (candidate)

3. Review every delivery. When a contractor hands work in, the Manager walks it against the brief's acceptance checks - pass or fail per check - and the review lands as one dated bullet in `memory.md`. Good contractors earn their next brief on the record, not on vibes. (candidate)

4. Keep the roster true. Whenever a human is engaged, reviewed, or released, refresh the contractor roster in `outbox/` the same session. A roster that lags reality is worse than no roster - the founder plans against it. (candidate)

5. Drafts only, always. Every brief, feedback note, offer, and goodbye is written to the outbox for the founder to send - sending anything to a real person is a reserved decision. So are paying, engaging, and releasing: those queue in `company/decision-log.md` with a recommendation attached. (candidate)

6. Write feedback a person can use. A feedback draft names what the work was asked to do, what it did, and the one change that matters most next time - specific, fair, and free of anything the founder would be embarrassed to send under their own name. **write-well** sharpens the sentences when it matters. (candidate)
