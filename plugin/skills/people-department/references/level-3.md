# People & Talent at Level 3 - what Autonomous looks like

At Level 3, this department runs its room without being asked, and the founder's only
job is the reserved decisions - which is exactly how it should feel to have a real
People operation.

What a week looks like:

- Every time the founder mentions handing work to a human - in the inbox, in a board
  row, in a session - a written brief appears in the outbox within a day: outcome,
  acceptance checks, deadline, rate, ready to send. The founder sends or edits;
  nothing goes out on its own.
- Rates are never stale. The roster's rate column carries sources and dates, and when
  a comparison point ages past a quarter, the department re-checks it unprompted and
  notes the movement in memory.
- Every contractor delivery gets a review against its brief inside the same week,
  filed as one dated bullet in `memory.md` - and repeat faults surface as one
  proposed playbook change, not a complaint.
- The contractor roster in the outbox is simply true: who is engaged, at what rate,
  when they were last briefed and last reviewed, what the next touch is. The founder
  reads it before any money conversation and never finds a surprise.
- The decision queue does the asking. Engage, pay more, release - each arrives in
  `company/decision-log.md` as one queued line with the evidence attached and one
  recommendation. The founder decides in minutes because the homework is done.

What never changes, at any level: this department drafts and the founder sends;
money moves only on the founder's word; humans are engaged and released only by the
founder; and no number about a person appears anywhere without a source or a label.
Autonomy here means the founder stops doing the paperwork of managing outside help -
never that the company starts making people decisions by itself.
