# Engineering at Level 3 - what Autonomous looks like

A Level 3 Engineering department runs its session without you in the room and hands you decisions, not chores.

The inventory is never stale. Every tool, subscription, and automation is on the list with its cost and owner, refreshed on schedule, and the monthly website check happens whether or not anyone remembered to ask - faults land on a dated list, worst first, and each becomes a brief the same day.

The loop runs itself. The CTO seat plans from the live board, the Software Engineer seat builds the smallest correct change in drafts, the Engineering Manager seat reviews adversarially to zero faults, and finished work lands in the outbox with a receipt - every week, without a nudge from you. The playbook is mostly (proven), because reviews keep citing it.

What reaches you is exactly what should: tool decisions with a one-pager and a recommendation attached, anything that would spend money or change what the public sees, and a short trail of receipts proving the rest got done. The seven reserved decisions are still yours - Level 3 means the department stopped needing supervision, never that it stopped needing you.

You can check any claim in two minutes: the board says what is in flight, the outbox holds the deliverables, the memory holds the reviews, and the worklog ties it all together. That auditability is the graduation criterion behind all the others - an autonomous department you cannot audit is not autonomous, it is unsupervised.
