# Engineering playbook

How this department does its work - a marker flips to (proven) only after a review cites the SOP in a passed deliverable.

1. The inventory pass. Before improving anything, know what exists: list the website and its pages, every tool and subscription with its monthly cost, and every automation with what it does and when it last ran. The founder supplies the facts; nothing is invented. An inventory older than 30 days gets refreshed before new work is planned. (candidate)

2. One small change at a time. Every brief scopes the smallest change that moves the outcome, and the builder makes exactly that change - no drive-by fixes, no "while I was in there". Two improvements are two briefs. (candidate)

3. Review to zero faults. No work reaches Done without an independent adversarial review: the reviewer re-runs, re-opens, and re-checks rather than trusting the handoff note, and passes only when a round comes back with zero faults. The review lands as one dated line in memory. (candidate)

4. The tool decision one-pager. When a tool is in question, the answer is one page: what job it does, what it costs, the honest alternatives, and a recommendation - keep, replace, or cancel. Anything that spends or cancels money queues to the founder in the decision log; the one-pager is the recommendation attached. (candidate)

5. Automate the third repetition. A chore done manually once is a chore; three times is a candidate for automation. Automations stay small, live in plain files the founder can read, and get run end-to-end in review before they count as working. (candidate)

6. The monthly website check. Walk the live site like a stranger: every link, every form, every page on a phone. Faults go on a dated list, worst first, and each becomes its own brief. When a fault is about what a page says rather than whether it works, the landing-pages craft owns the fix. (candidate)

7. Every change leaves a receipt. No change to the site, a tool, or an automation is finished until a receipt names what was produced, where it lives, and who reviewed it. A change nobody can trace is a future mystery, and this department does not ship mysteries. (candidate)
