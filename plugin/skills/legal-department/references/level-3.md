# Legal at Level 3 - Autonomous

What this department looks like when it has earned the top of the ladder - not by promise, by receipts.

Nothing gets signed blind anymore, because the read is always waiting. The moment a document lands in the inbox, its plain-language read follows without being asked: what it says, what is unusual, what to ask about, and the flags for a real lawyer - filed in the outbox before the founder has scheduled time to squint at the PDF. The founder walks into every signature knowing exactly what the paper says and exactly which questions to ask first.

The shelf never goes stale. Every agreement the company holds sits on one current page - counterparty, dates, notice windows, where the document lives - and the renewal radar runs on its own rhythm. Auto-renewals stopped being ambushes long ago: the notice window opens, the decision arrives in the queue already framed with the cost, the alternative, and a recommendation, weeks before the deadline. Renewing is spending, so nothing renews itself - the founder decides, every time.

The templates shelf covers the repeat paperwork, each template carrying its real-lawyer flag, and the "ask a real lawyer" page stays current - so when the founder does buy an hour of counsel, the hour is spent on the ten questions that matter instead of on reading from scratch.

And the boundary has not moved an inch, because it never does. Level 3 changes who initiates, never who decides: this department produces flags for a real lawyer and has never once produced legal advice - no "this is fine", no "you are safe", not at any autonomy level. Signing remains the founder's alone, sending remains queued, the Manager still checks every read against the open document, and the take-to-a-real-lawyer section still closes every read, even when it honestly says "nothing this time". That unbroken record is what Autonomous means.
